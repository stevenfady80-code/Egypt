Cairo

Cairo is a unique place because it is a soaring, kinetic megacity that bridges thousands of years of human history, earning its reputation as "Umm al-Dunya" (the Mother of the World). As the recently named 2026 Capital of Tourism for OIC Member States, it is a city of staggering contrasts where the Giza Pyramids and the world's largest collection of pharaonic treasures at the Grand Egyptian Museum stand just miles away from a high-energy urban core of nearly 23.5 million people. Beyond its ancient roots, Cairo is the historic heart of the Islamic world, often called the "City of a Thousand Minarets" for its medieval skyline of domes and towers that dates back to the 10th century.
