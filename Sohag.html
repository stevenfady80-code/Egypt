Sohag

Sohag is a unique place because it serves as a spiritual and archaeological cornerstone of Middle Egypt, remaining largely untouched by the heavy footprints of mass tourism. Known historically as a center for early Christian asceticism, it is home to two of the most significant Coptic monuments in the world—the White Monastery and the Red Monastery—the latter of which features some of the best-preserved Byzantine-era wall paintings in existence. The city acts as a bridge to the ancient past, sitting near the sacred site of Abydos, the cult center of Osiris and home to the Temple of Seti I, famous for its exquisite high-relief carvings. With its sprawling Nile-side corniche and deep-rooted agricultural traditions, Sohag offers an authentic glimpse into the soul of Upper Egypt, where ancient heritage and local Coptic and Islamic traditions coexist in a quiet, powerful harmony
