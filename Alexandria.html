Alexandria

Alexandria is a unique place because it serves as Egyptâ€™s "Bride of the Mediterranean," a city that feels more like a cosmopolitan European port than a traditional Middle Eastern metropolis. Founded by Alexander the Great in 331 BC, it was once the intellectual heart of the world, housing the Great Library of Alexandria and the Pharos Lighthouse, one of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World. Today, that legacy lives on in the strikingly modern Bibliotheca Alexandrina, while the Citadel of Qaitbay stands defiantly on the very foundation of the lost lighthouse. Walking along its sweeping Corniche provides a sensory blend of salty sea breezes, centuries-old Greek and Italian architecture, and a vibrant cafe culture that has inspired legendary writers and poets for millennia.
