Minya

Minya is a unique place because it serves as the "Bride of Upper Egypt," a vital cultural bridge between the north and south that preserves some of the most radical and experimental moments in human history. It is home to Tell el-Amarna, the short-lived 14th-century BC capital where Pharaoh Akhenaten launched the world's first recorded monotheistic revolution. Beyond this visionary city, the region boasts the rock-cut tombs of Beni Hasan, which contain vibrant Middle Kingdom murals depicting daily life with such detail that they include rare scenes of ancient wrestling and barbers at work.
