Giza

Giza is a unique place because it is the only location on Earth where the last standing Wonder of the Ancient World—the Great Pyramid of Khufu—exists in a modern urban landscape. While many view it as an extension of Cairo, Giza is a distinct city on the Nile's west bank that serves as the ultimate threshold between the fertile valley and the vast Sahara. Its skyline is dominated by the Giza Plateau, an architectural marvel of mathematical precision where three massive pyramids and the enigmatic Great Sphinx have guarded the desert for over 4,500 years. Today, this ancient majesty is meeting the future with the soft opening of the Grand Egyptian Museum (GEM), a colossal cultural hub that is poised to become the largest museum in the world dedicated to a single civilization, housing the complete, legendary treasures of Tutankhamun.
