Faiyum

Faiyum is a unique place because it is Egypt's oldest city and largest natural oasis, a lush 6,000-square-kilometer depression that functions as a living time capsule of geological and human history. It is home to the world-renowned Wadi Al-Hitan (Valley of Whales), a UNESCO World Heritage site where desert sands preserve 40-million-year-old fossils of whales with hind limbs, proving their evolutionary journey from land to sea. This prehistoric landscape is balanced by the bohemian charm of Tunis Village, an artistic colony overlooking the ancient Lake Qarun that has become Egyptâ€™s premier center for handcrafted pottery. From the rare desert waterfalls of Wadi El Rayan to the iconic 12th-century wooden waterwheels that still irrigate the land, Faiyum offers a rare blend of deep-time paleontology, Greco-Roman ruins, and modern eco-tourism just a short drive from the chaos of Cairo.
